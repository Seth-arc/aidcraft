About Debt & Diplomacy

Educational Purpose

Debt & Diplomacy is an educational simulation designed as a learning experience design project to help users understand the complex dynamics of development finance in emerging economies. The scenarios presented are based on real-world situations faced by many nations.

Key Learning Objectives

  • Understand debt sustainability challenges in infrastructure financing
  • Navigate complex stakeholder relationships including domestic and international actors
  • Balance economic development with environmental and social considerations
  • Experience the trade-offs involved in fiscal policy decision-making

This simulation is designed for educational purposes and simplifies complex economic and political realities. It should be viewed as an introduction to these concepts rather than a comprehensive representation.

Development Finance Concepts

Hidden Debt Challenge

Your coastal nation of Azuria secured a commercial loan from China to build a major port facility, promising increased trade and economic growth. As repayment deadlines approach, the port is operating at only 40% capacity, falling far short of revenue projections. Initial analysis suggests the loan agreement contains concerning collateral clauses that could transfer control to the lender in case of default, while local fishing communities report significant environmental damage from port dredging operations.

Development Finance Context

This scenario reflects issues faced by many countries engaging in large infrastructure projects with foreign financing. The "hidden debt" challenge refers to loan terms that may not be fully transparent, potentially including asset-based collateral or management control provisions in case of default.

Key Challenges

  • Find refinancing options for a port that is generating insufficient revenue to meet the upcoming loan payment
  • Civil society organizations are demanding full loan term disclosure, threatening political stability
  • Fishing communities are threatening protests due to environmental impacts
  • Balancing diplomatic relations with various local and international stakeholders

Success Criteria

  • Establish a sustainable debt repayment plan
  • Mitigate environmental concerns without compromising fiscal stability
  • Maintain diplomatic relations with all stakeholders
  • Keep political influence above the critical threshold

Game Guide

Your Mission

Balance fiscal responsibility, diplomatic relationships, and political priorities to safeguard your nation's financial stability while managing complex development finance projects.

Key Metrics

Fiscal Sustainability

Measures your ability to maintain financial health by balancing revenue generation, debt service, and operational expenses. Low fiscal sustainability increases default risk and may trigger loan collateral clauses.

Environmental Compliance

Reflects how well you're addressing ecological impacts from infrastructure projects. Higher compliance improves community relations and access to certain financing options, while low compliance risks protests and operational disruption.

Political Capital

Your influence with domestic stakeholders and government officials. Critical for implementing difficult reforms and maintaining your position. If this drops too low, you may be removed from office, ending the game.

International Relations

Your diplomatic standing with global financial institutions and bilateral partners. Balancing relations with different international stakeholders opens more financing options and prevents geopolitical complications.

Stakeholder Management

Each stakeholder has their own interests, influence level, and relationship status with you. Building and maintaining positive relationships opens new opportunities and improves metrics, while hostile relationships can create significant challenges.

Relationship Status Effects:
  • Friendly: Provides bonuses to related metrics, may unlock special options, and increases chances of successful negotiations
  • Neutral: Standard interactions with no special bonuses or penalties
  • Hostile: Significantly reduces related metrics, blocks certain options, and may trigger negative events
Engagement Tips:
  • Carefully consider each stakeholder's key interests before taking actions that affect them
  • Additional interactions with stakeholders can improve relationships but may consume resources
  • Prioritize relationships based on stakeholder influence and alignment with your objectives
  • Remember that it's impossible to keep all stakeholders happy simultaneously - strategic trade-offs are essential

Resource Management

You have limited resources to manage throughout the game:

Budget

Financial resources for implementing actions. Different actions have different costs, and when your budget is depleted, your options become severely limited.

Staff

Your team members who implement actions. Each action may require staff commitment, temporarily reducing available staff until the action is completed.

Political Influence

Your personal political capital that can be spent on difficult decisions. This resource is particularly hard to replenish and must be managed carefully.

Time

Each action consumes days, and you have a limited timeframe to resolve the crisis before loan repayments are due.

Strategic Advice

  • Begin by thoroughly reviewing all available documents to understand the full context
  • Balance short-term gains against long-term sustainability
  • Monitor all four key metrics carefully - if any drop too low, you may face game-ending consequences
  • Diversify your approaches rather than focusing exclusively on one aspect of the challenge
  • When stakeholder interests conflict, try to find compromise solutions that address core concerns
  • Remember that transparency with stakeholders typically yields better outcomes than concealment
