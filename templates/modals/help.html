Welcome to AidCraft Workshop Simulation

This simulation explores the challenges of development finance and hidden debt. Here's how to get started:

Simulation Overview

AidCraft is divided into four phases:

  1. Analysis Phase: Understand the development context and stakeholders
  2. Funding Phase: Evaluate and select funding options
  3. Negotiation Phase: Engage with stakeholders to build agreements
  4. Outcome Phase: See the consequences of your decisions

Basic Controls

  • Navigate between phases using the buttons at the bottom of the screen
  • Monitor your resources in the sidebar
  • Keep an eye on the timer - decisions may have time pressure
  • Click on stakeholders to learn more about them and engage

Tips for Success

  • Balance stakeholder needs with sustainable development goals
  • Pay attention to the terms and conditions of funding options
  • Be aware of hidden debt risks in financing arrangements
  • Build strategic stakeholder relationships to create coalitions

Phase Guide

Analysis Phase

In this initial phase, you'll assess the development context and understand stakeholder dynamics.

  • Review stakeholder profiles and understand their interests
  • Analyze the development needs and priorities
  • Map the initial political landscape
  • Assess resources and constraints

Funding Phase

This phase is about evaluating and selecting funding options for your development project.

  • Compare funding sources (international, domestic, private)
  • Assess interest rates, terms, and conditions
  • Watch for hidden debt potential in agreements
  • Allocate your budget across priorities
  • Monitor risk assessments for your choices

Negotiation Phase

Here you'll engage with stakeholders to build support and manage opposition.

  • Conduct bilateral negotiations with key stakeholders
  • Make concessions to build goodwill
  • Form coalitions among aligned stakeholders
  • Manage difficult relationships to minimize opposition
  • Respond to events and unexpected developments

Outcome Phase

The final phase reveals the consequences of your decisions throughout the simulation.

  • Review project success metrics
  • See the revealed hidden debt impact
  • Assess stakeholder satisfaction and relationships
  • Explore long-term impact projections
  • Reflect on key insights and lessons learned

Controls & Interface

Navigation

  • Phase Navigation: Use the "Previous Phase" and "Next Phase" buttons at the bottom of the screen
  • Phase Sidebar: Click on unlocked phases in the sidebar to jump to that phase

Key Concepts

Hidden Debt

Hidden debt refers to financial obligations that are not immediately visible in official debt statistics. These can include:

  • Contingent liabilities in contract clauses
  • Non-transparent loan terms and conditions
  • Off-balance sheet arrangements
  • State-owned enterprise borrowing
  • Public-private partnerships with unfavorable risk allocation

These hidden obligations can create unexpected fiscal burdens in the future.

Stakeholder Dynamics

Stakeholders have varying interests, levels of influence, and relationships with each other:

  • Interests: What a stakeholder wants from the development project
  • Influence: How much power a stakeholder has to affect outcomes
  • Stance: Whether a stakeholder is supportive, neutral, or opposed
  • Relationships: How stakeholders interact with each other (allied, neutral, opposed)

Sustainable Development

Sustainable development balances immediate needs with long-term viability:

  • Economic Sustainability: Financially viable over time
  • Social Sustainability: Benefits widely shared across society
  • Environmental Sustainability: Minimizes negative environmental impacts
  • Institutional Sustainability: Creates or strengthens enduring institutions

Development Finance

The various sources and instruments used to fund development projects:

  • Official Development Assistance (ODA): Grants and concessional loans from donor countries
  • Domestic Resources: Tax revenue, bonds, and national development banks
  • International Financial Institutions: World Bank, regional development banks
  • Commercial Financing: Market-rate loans, private investment
  • Mixed Financing: Blended finance, public-private partnerships
