Glossary of Terms

Hidden Debt

Financial obligations that are not transparent or fully disclosed in official debt statistics. These can include contingent liabilities, off-balance sheet arrangements, and debt with non-transparent terms and conditions.

Concessional Financing

Loans that are extended on terms substantially more generous than market loans. The concessionality is achieved either through interest rates below market rates, grace periods, or a combination of these.

Debt Sustainability

The ability of a country to meet its debt obligations without requiring debt relief or accumulating arrears. It involves balancing the need for development financing with the burden of debt service payments.

Contingent Liability

A potential financial obligation that depends on the occurrence of an uncertain future event. In development projects, these often appear in contract clauses, guarantees, or insurance arrangements.

Public-Private Partnership (PPP)

A cooperative arrangement between public and private sectors for financing, building, and operating infrastructure or services. May involve complex risk allocation and potentially hidden liabilities for the public sector.

Stakeholder

An individual, group, or organization with an interest in, or affected by, a development project. Stakeholders may include government entities, financial institutions, civil society, and local communities.

Coalition

A temporary alliance of distinct stakeholders who pool their resources and support to achieve a common objective. In development projects, coalitions can significantly influence outcomes.

Influence Level

The degree to which a stakeholder can affect the decisions, processes, or outcomes of a development project, based on their power, legitimacy, or urgency of their claims.

Stakeholder Mapping

The process of identifying and analyzing stakeholders, their interests, influence, and interrelationships. Used for strategic planning and managing stakeholder engagements.

Sustainable Development

Development that meets the needs of the present without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs. Encompasses economic, social, and environmental sustainability.

Official Development Assistance (ODA)

Government aid designed to promote the economic development and welfare of developing countries. Can include grants, concessional loans, and technical assistance.

Project Implementation Unit (PIU)

A designated team responsible for managing and implementing a development project. May operate within existing government structures or as a separate entity.

Development Finance Institution (DFI)

Specialized financial institutions that support private sector development in developing countries. Examples include the International Finance Corporation (IFC) and regional development banks.

Analysis Phase

The first phase of the AidCraft simulation, focusing on understanding the development context, stakeholder dynamics, and initial assessment of needs and resources.

Funding Phase

The second phase of the AidCraft simulation, where participants evaluate and select from various funding options with different terms, conditions, and potential hidden debt implications.

Negotiation Phase

The third phase of the AidCraft simulation, focused on engaging with stakeholders, building agreements, forming coalitions, and managing opposition.

Outcome Phase

The final phase of the AidCraft simulation, revealing the consequences of decisions, including project success metrics, stakeholder satisfaction, and hidden debt impacts.

Political Capital

A resource in the AidCraft simulation representing influence, goodwill, and political support. Used to enable certain actions, particularly in stakeholder negotiations.
